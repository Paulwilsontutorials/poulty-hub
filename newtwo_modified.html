Pre-requisite Knowledge: Embryology of the Chicken

Understanding normal vs. abnormal embryo development is crucial for identifying and addressing mortality causes during incubation.

What is a Hatchery?

A hatchery is a facility for egg storage, grading, fumigation, incubation, and hatching, designed to ensure optimal environmental control for embryo development. It is basically a facility designed for the incubation and hatching of poultry eggs, (primarily chickens), to produce chicks for large or small-scale production.

Factors in Establishing a Hatchery

When establishing a hatchery in Malawi, several factors must be taken into account to ensure: Efficiency, Productivity, Sustainability

  • Market Demand: Analyze local needs for broilers, layers, and indigenous chicks.
  • Capital: Budget for land, equipment, utilities, and staffing.
  • Location: Accessibility, utilities, biosecurity, and regulatory compliance.
  • Workforce: Skilled personnel for incubation and chick handling.
  • Size: Capacity planning for setters and hatchers.

Hatchery Construction & Layout

Design separate rooms for clean and dirty operations: receiving, fumigation, setting, hatching, grading, and dispatch.

  • Ventilation, drainage, and hygienic surfaces
  • Power backup (generators/solar)
  • Biosecurity: footbaths, entry control, change rooms

Determination of Hatchery Size

it depends on

  • Capacity of setters and hatchers in the incubator i.e. maximum number of eggs that can be set o incubated at one time eg 5,000.
  • Number of eggs incubated or set/wk: 4000 eggs set /week
  • Number of chicks hatched per wk/setting: 3,800 chicks hatched per week

Key Equipment & Systems

  • Incubators (setters, hatchers) with temperature, humidity, and automatic turning
  • Candlers for embryo monitoring
  • Chick processing: grading, vaccination stations
  • Disinfection: foggers, UV, quats
  • Waste management: composting, incineration, rendering

Hatchery Management

From egg handling and storage to pulling the hatch and chick take-off:

  1. Egg storage (max 14 days at 18°C, 75% RH)
  2. Incubation controls: temp, ventilation, humidity, egg position, turning
  3. Pulling the hatch vs. take-off process
  4. Quality chick criteria and dispatch protocols

Records & KPIs

Maintain detailed logs for source, incubation, hatchability, chick quality, biosecurity, and sales to optimize performance.

  • Fertility Rate (90–95%)
  • Hatchability Rate (80–85%)
  • Chick Quality (>95% healthy chicks)
